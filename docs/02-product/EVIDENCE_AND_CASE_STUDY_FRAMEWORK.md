# Evidence and Case Study Framework

Status: Phase 1 working baseline; implementation-neutral
Owner: Project owner
Scope: Public evidence for the live Pixel&Digital and Kwaliti Print MVP worlds. Studio and Training may appear only as approved teaser/foundation content.

## 1. Purpose

Make every material public claim traceable, rights-cleared and useful to a visitor's decision. Absence of proof is a visible content gap, never permission to invent clients, claims, metrics, names, prices, dates, budgets, legal facts or capabilities.

## 2. Evidence classes

| Class | Acceptable basis | Required control | Public use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Deliverable | Approved work artifact or documented output | Ownership, client permission and usage context | Show what was produced without implying results |
| Outcome | Attributable qualitative or quantitative result | Source, period/context, calculation basis and approver | State only the verified scope and limitations |
| Before/after | Comparable approved states | Provenance, fair comparison and explanatory caption | Do not imply causation beyond evidence |
| Testimonial | Client-approved statement | Speaker authority, exact approval and publication rights | Preserve meaning; identify attribution level |
| Process evidence | Documented method or production step | Operational owner confirms current accuracy | Proves approach, not unmeasured impact |
| Capability evidence | Real example tied to a confirmed capability | Operational validation, context, scale and constraints | Do not imply inventory, capacity, lead time or feasibility |

## 3. Claim and evidence record

Each claim must record:

- exact proposed wording, owning world, accountable claim owner and intended placement;
- evidence class, source location, source owner and verification date;
- client/project attribution permission and any anonymisation requirement;
- metric definition, baseline, period, scope, method and limitations when applicable;
- media rights, credits, permitted channels, expiry or withdrawal constraints;
- personal or confidential information present and required legal/privacy review;
- accessible alternative: alt text, caption, transcript or adjacent explanation;
- mobile treatment and the meaning retained without hover, motion, audio, 3D or wide comparison;
- performance treatment: critical proof, lightweight preview/fallback and optional enhancement;
- related service/capability and the conversion action the proof supports;
- status: `Proposed`, `Evidence pending`, `Rights pending`, `In review`, `Approved`, `Published` or `Withdrawn`.

## 4. Case study minimum structure

1. Context and verified challenge, with confidential details excluded.
2. Approved scope and contributing business units; name one lead owner for cross-unit work.
3. Work or process evidence that can be publicly shown.
4. Attributable outcome, or an explicit qualitative/deliverable-only treatment when no valid metric exists.
5. Media with scale/context, rights, credits and accessible equivalents.
6. Related confirmed services or Kwaliti Print capabilities.
7. One appropriate similar-project, quotation or enquiry action with no unapproved promise.

## 5. Review and publication gate

The case-study owner assembles the record; the operational owner verifies capability and method; the client/rights approver confirms attribution and media use; the content owner checks clarity, mobile order and conversion relevance; accessibility and performance reviewers check equivalent meaning and delivery risk. Legal/privacy review is mandatory where consent, personal data, confidentiality, regulated claims or retention obligations are implicated.

Publication is blocked when a material claim lacks a source, a metric lacks context, rights are unclear, confidentiality is unresolved, enhanced media has no equivalent, or the call to action implies an unapproved price, response time, availability or capability.

## 6. Maintenance and withdrawal

- Assign a review trigger for changed capability, expired permission, corrected data or client withdrawal.
- Archive rather than silently reuse evidence outside its approved context.
- Remove or correct public material promptly when its source, rights or accuracy no longer hold.
- Preserve an approval and change history without exposing sensitive source material publicly.

## 7. Open owner decisions

- Which clients, projects, testimonials, outcomes and media may be named or shown.
- Who may grant client attribution, anonymisation and media-use approval.
- Which launch claims and ODR-003-approved current Kwaliti Print capabilities have sufficient operational evidence; future-only capabilities must not be treated as launch evidence targets.
- Whether approved evidence is sufficient for each live-world journey and, only if ODR-021 permits it, for Insights inclusion.
- Legal identity, privacy terms, consent wording, confidentiality rules and retention requirements.
- Launch languages/geographies, which affect approvals and contextual accuracy.
- Media-production budget and availability; missing proof remains a gap.
