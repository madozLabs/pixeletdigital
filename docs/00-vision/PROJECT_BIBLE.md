# Pixel&Digital — Project Bible

Status: Foundational draft
Owner: Project owner
Technical authority: CTO operating under `AGENTS.md`
Repository: `madozLabs/pixeletdigital`

## 1. Purpose

Pixel&Digital is a communication and marketing agency that sells expertise while operating with the discipline and tooling of a technology company.

The product is not limited to a public website. It is a unified platform with two faces:

1. a premium public experience that presents the agency and converts visitors into qualified prospects;
2. an internal operating system that centralizes content, commercial activity, production and collaboration.

The public website is the showcase. The administration platform is the operational engine.

## 2. Mission

Help organizations strengthen their visual, digital and commercial presence through an integrated ecosystem of strategy, creativity, technology, production and training services.

## 3. Long-term vision

Pixel&Digital should become a leading modern communication company whose competitive advantage comes from both the quality of its expertise and the quality of its internal execution.

The agency remains a service business. The software platform exists to improve delivery, consistency, speed, traceability and team productivity; it is not the primary product sold to the market.

## 4. Brand architecture

Pixel&Digital is the parent brand and the common platform owner.

The ecosystem contains distinct business units with independent visual identities, narratives and experiences while sharing the same technical foundation, data governance and administration.

### 4.1 Pixel&Digital

Role: parent agency and digital communication brand.

Core domains:

- digital strategy;
- social media management;
- community management;
- Meta, TikTok and LinkedIn advertising;
- content strategy and creation;
- branding and graphic design;
- motion design and video editing;
- websites and web platforms.

Experience direction:

- dominant red identity;
- modern, confident and precise;
- strong sense of movement and transformation;
- premium without visual excess.

### 4.2 Kwaliti Print

Role: printing, personalization, signage and fabrication business unit.

Current and planned domains:

- personalized promotional items;
- banner printing;
- vinyl printing;
- stickers and event materials;
- signage;
- three-dimensional lettering;
- CNC-assisted cutting and fabrication.

Experience direction:

- predominantly white identity;
- material, texture, precision and workshop atmosphere;
- immersive transitions and optional lightweight 3D when it improves understanding;
- clear product discovery and quotation paths despite the visual immersion.

Kwaliti Print must feel like a distinct brand, not a themed subsection of Pixel&Digital.

### 4.3 Audiovisual studio

Working name: to be defined.

Role: photo, video and audiovisual production business unit.

Domains:

- studio photography;
- product photography;
- corporate photography;
- video production;
- interviews and podcasts;
- advertising content;
- event coverage;
- editing and post-production.

Experience direction:

- red may remain part of the identity, but the visual language must be cinematic;
- focus on light, framing, sound, motion and storytelling;
- the final name and identity require owner approval.

### 4.4 Training unit

Working name: to be defined.

Role: professional and practical training.

Potential domains:

- digital marketing;
- community management;
- graphic design;
- web development;
- advertising platforms;
- video editing and motion design;
- generative AI and productivity.

Experience direction:

- blue-oriented identity;
- accessible, optimistic and structured;
- emphasis on progression, competence and community;
- future support for schedules, registration, payments and certificates.

## 5. Public experience objectives

The public platform must achieve four outcomes.

### 5.1 Impress

The first interaction must demonstrate digital and creative mastery. Motion, 3D and immersive techniques may be used when they remain responsive, accessible and purposeful.

### 5.2 Explain

Visitors must quickly understand the agency, each business unit, the services offered and the next action available. Immersion must never reduce comprehension.

### 5.3 Prove

The platform must prioritize evidence:

- case studies;
- measurable outcomes;
- before-and-after presentations;
- campaign performance;
- websites delivered;
- visual identities;
- audiovisual productions;
- printed and fabricated products;
- client testimonials.

### 5.4 Convert

Each journey must lead naturally to one or more business actions:

- request a quotation;
- book a consultation;
- send a project brief;
- register for training;
- contact a specific business unit.

## 6. Experience principles

- Multiple brand worlds, one coherent ecosystem.
- Each business unit has its own palette, typography rules, motion rhythm, imagery and tone.
- Shared navigation and transition patterns preserve orientation.
- Spectacle must serve meaning and conversion.
- Mobile and low-performance devices are first-class constraints.
- Three-dimensional content requires progressive enhancement and a non-3D fallback.
- Accessibility, loading speed and legibility are release requirements.
- The site should feel curated like a showroom, not structured like a catalogue.

## 7. Internal platform vision

The administration should evolve into Pixel&Digital's operating system.

It should ultimately support:

- multi-brand content management;
- prospects, clients and commercial opportunities;
- quotations and follow-up actions;
- projects, tasks, owners and deadlines;
- production workflows;
- Kwaliti Print requests and jobs;
- studio scheduling and productions;
- training sessions, registrations and learners;
- media and document management;
- dashboards and operational reporting;
- users, roles and permissions;
- audit history for sensitive actions.

The platform should feel like a focused modern SaaS product, not a generic admin template.

### Internal

## 9. Product pillars

### 9.1 Premium public presence

A fast, immersive and conversion-oriented multi-brand experience.

### 9.2 Structured content management

Teams can update content without breaking brand rules or page composition.

### 9.3 Lightweight commercial operations

Leads, opportunities, ownership, next actions and history are centralized.

### 9.4 Delivery operations

Projects, production work, responsibilities and deliverables become traceable.

### 9.5 Business-unit autonomy

Each unit manages its own relevant content and workflows while the Super Admin retains global control.

### 9.6 Evidence and reporting

The system should support decision-making through reliable metrics rather than vanity dashboards.

## 10. MVP direction

The first release should not attempt to implement the complete operating system.

Recommended MVP scope:

1. public Pixel&Digital website;
2. distinct Kwaliti Print public world;
3. foundations for future Studio and Training worlds;
4. structured CMS for services, projects, case studies, articles, team, testimonials and global settings;
5. contact, quotation and consultation requests;
6. lightweight lead tracking;
7. user, role and permission foundations;
8. media management;
9. SEO, accessibility, performance and analytics foundations.

The MVP must prove the brand experience, content workflow and commercial conversion path before broader operational modules are built.

## 11. Explicit non-goals for the MVP

- internal chat or social network;
- video conferencing;
- full accounting ERP;
- payroll or complete human resources management;
- generic workflow engine;
- replacement for every external productivity tool;
- native mobile application;
- unrestricted page builder;
- microservice architecture without demonstrated need.

## 12. Content model principles

- Content is structured, not free-form by default.
- Each content object has an owning business unit.
- Cross-unit case studies can reference contributions from multiple units.
- Publishing follows the canonical lifecycle: Draft, In review, Scheduled (optional), Published and Archived. Rejection returns content to Draft.
- Public URLs and metadata must be stable and SEO-aware.
- Media assets require ownership, alternative text and usage context.
- Sensitive operational data is separate from public content.

## 13. Success criteria

The project succeeds when:

- visitors immediately understand Pixel&Digital's value;
- each business unit is memorable and clearly differentiated;
- the experience remains understandable on mobile;
- qualified enquiries increase and their sources are traceable;
- internal teams can manage content without developer intervention;
- the Super Admin can govern users, brands and publication;
- the technical foundation can add operational modules without redesigning the product from zero.

## 14. Open owner decisions

The following require owner input before final branding or launch decisions:

- final name of the audiovisual studio;
- final name of the training unit;
- official taglines and market positioning language;
- exact launch service catalogue and pricing visibility;
- target launch geography and languages;
- whether consultation booking uses an external calendar provider;
- legal company information, privacy terms and data-retention requirements;
- production budget for photography, video, 3D assets and original sound.

These decisions do not block continued product discovery, information architecture or workflow definition.

## 15. Controlled documentation set and next gate

The vision, brand architecture, MVP scope, roles and permissions, public and internal journeys, roadmap, brand bibles, information architecture, motion guidelines and wireframe specifications form the controlled product baseline under `docs`.

The next gate is owner review of the open brand, market, legal, scope and launch decisions, followed by completion of the remaining Phase 1 deliverables identified in the roadmap. No application architecture or production code should be approved until that review explicitly authorizes Phase 2.
