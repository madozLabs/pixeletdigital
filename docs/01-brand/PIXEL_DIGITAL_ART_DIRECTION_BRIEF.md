# Pixel&Digital — Art Direction Brief

Status: Directional brief for exploration; not final art direction

## 1. Desired impression

Pixel&Digital should feel modern, confident, precise, creative and commercially credible. The experience must demonstrate digital mastery without becoming noisy, trendy for its own sake or difficult to understand.

## 2. Visual narrative

- Start with a clear business promise, then reveal expertise, process and evidence.
- Treat red as an energetic signature, not a permanent full-screen requirement.
- Combine disciplined editorial composition with purposeful movement.
- Prefer real people, real work, real processes and real outcomes over generic stock.
- Show the agency as one integrated capability across strategy, creativity, technology and production.

## 3. Image direction

Priority subjects: team at work, creative process, production detail, client-approved deliverables, campaign or web evidence, studio equipment only where truthful, and cross-unit collaboration.

Avoid: staged handshake imagery, anonymous laptops, fake dashboards, decorative neon, unsupported “future tech” visuals and any image that implies an unconfirmed capability.

## 4. Motion direction

Motion may explain connection, transformation, sequencing or movement between universes. It must never delay reading, hide navigation, hijack scrolling or force sound. Storyboards are reviewed before production.

## 5. Composition

- Strong hierarchy, deliberate whitespace and asymmetric editorial rhythm are encouraged.
- Primary actions remain obvious and stable.
- Case-study evidence sits near the claim it supports.
- Mobile uses a deliberate linear narrative rather than a compressed desktop composition.

## 6. Brand-world boundary

Pixel&Digital remains the parent red-led world. Kwaliti Print must retain its predominantly white, tactile and production-oriented identity. Studio and Training remain teaser/foundation scope only until names and identities are approved.

## 7. Production gate

No final shoot, illustration system, motion package, sound identity or original 3D production begins until subject, purpose, rights, budget, mobile treatment, accessible equivalent, performance allowance and accountable owner are approved.
