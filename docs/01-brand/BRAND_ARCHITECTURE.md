# Pixel&Digital — Brand Architecture

Status: Working draft
Parent reference: `docs/00-vision/PROJECT_BIBLE.md`

## 1. Decision

Pixel&Digital will use an endorsed multi-brand model.

Pixel&Digital is the parent brand and platform owner. Kwaliti Print, the future audiovisual studio and the future training unit are distinct business-unit brands with their own public experiences.

They are not separate technical products by default. They share governance, administration, data standards and selected platform capabilities.

## 2. Brand relationship model

### Parent brand

Pixel&Digital provides:

- trust and corporate legitimacy;
- shared operating standards;
- common account and administration infrastructure;
- cross-selling between capabilities;
- consolidated case studies and client relationships.

### Business-unit brands

Each business unit owns:

- a distinct visual language;
- a focused value proposition;
- a service catalogue;
- relevant case studies and media;
- its conversion journeys;
- its operational workflows inside the shared administration.

A unit may be presented as “by Pixel&Digital” where commercial trust benefits from the endorsement, but the endorsement must not visually dominate the unit’s identity.

## 3. Shared family traits

The ecosystem must remain recognizable as one family through:

- high production quality;
- confident and concise language;
- deliberate motion;
- strong typography;
- clear conversion actions;
- consistent accessibility and performance standards;
- coherent navigation between universes;
- a common standard for evidence and case studies.

The brands do not need to share the same colours or component styling.

## 4. Pixel&Digital world

### Strategic role

Parent agency, digital strategy and creative technology.

### Core promise

Transform brands through strategy, creativity, digital execution and measurable communication.

### Personality

- bold;
- intelligent;
- inventive;
- precise;
- energetic;
- business-aware.

### Visual direction

- red as the dominant signature;
- controlled contrast;
- editorial layouts;
- kinetic typography and purposeful transitions;
- digital materials rather than ornamental effects;
- premium restraint.

### Motion direction

Motion communicates acceleration, connection and transformation. It should be responsive and never delay comprehension.

## 5. Kwaliti Print world

### Strategic role

Long-term brand role: physical production, printing, personalization, signage and fabrication. This strategic description is not a claim that every capability is currently available.

Current-status boundary: Personalized Gadgets is the only currently stated available Kwaliti Print capability. Banner Printing, Vinyl Printing and 3D Lettering/CNC are future-only; other detailed capabilities remain unconfirmed pending explicit owner decisions.

### Core promise

Turn ideas and identities into precise, tangible objects and visual surfaces.

### Personality

- exact;
- practical;
- tactile;
- dependable;
- inventive;
- production-focused.

### Visual direction

- predominantly white identity;
- deep neutral accents as needed;
- close-up materials, surfaces, inks and edges;
- technical grids and measurement cues;
- high-quality product photography;
- optional 3D product or fabrication scenes.

### Motion direction

Motion evokes layers, cutting, folding, printing, assembly and transformation of material.

### Usability constraint

The user must always find services, formats, examples and quotation actions without having to decode the immersive experience.

## 6. Audiovisual studio world

### Status

Name and final identity pending owner decision.

### Strategic role

Photo, video, sound and audiovisual storytelling.

### Core promise

Create images and narratives that make brands visible, memorable and emotionally credible.

### Personality

- cinematic;
- observant;
- expressive;
- disciplined;
- contemporary.

### Visual direction

- dark and light contrasts inspired by studio lighting;
- red may remain as an accent or signature;
- frame-based compositions;
- immersive full-bleed media;
- restrained interface chrome around the work.

### Motion direction

Motion evokes focus pulls, cuts, reveals, light changes and timelines. Autoplay media must be controlled and accessible.

## 7. Training world

### Status

Name and final identity pending owner decision.

### Strategic role

Practical professional learning and capability development.

### Core promise

Turn knowledge into usable competence through structured, applied training.

### Personality

- clear;
- encouraging;
- practical;
- progressive;
- credible;
- community-oriented.

### Visual direction

- blue-led identity;
- bright and structured layouts;
- visual indicators of progression;
- human learning imagery;
- accessible information density.

### Motion direction

Motion communicates progression, unlocking, sequencing and achievement.

## 8. Navigation between worlds

The system must clearly tell visitors:

- which world they are currently in;
- how to return to Pixel&Digital;
- how to discover the other business units;
- whether a service belongs to one or several units.

Recommended principles:

- a shared ecosystem switcher or “Our worlds” entry;
- world-specific local navigation;
- controlled transition sequences between worlds;
- persistent access to contact or quotation actions;
- no hidden navigation solely for artistic effect.

## 9. Cross-unit case studies

Case studies are ecosystem evidence, not isolated portfolio entries.

A project may identify contributions such as:

- strategy by Pixel&Digital;
- branding and social content by the creative team;
- photography or video by the studio;
- printing and fabrication by Kwaliti Print;
- client training by the training unit.

The data model must eventually support multiple contributing units and measurable outcomes.

## 10. Design-system implication

The future design system should have two layers:

1. shared foundations: accessibility, spacing logic, interaction states, responsiveness, content structure and technical primitives;
2. brand themes: typography, colours, surfaces, motion tokens, imagery rules and selected component expressions.

This does not authorize technical implementation yet. It establishes the product requirement.

## 11. Brand governance

The Super Admin governs all brands.

Delegated users may be assigned to specific business units. A user must not automatically gain access to every brand’s unpublished content or operational data.

Changes to core brand tokens, navigation or public identity require elevated permission and audit history.

## 12. Open decisions

- final names of Studio and Training;
- exact endorsement wording for each business unit;
- definitive logo assets and usage rules;
- approved type families;
- final colour values;
- sound identity policy;
- budget and production method for original 3D assets;
- whether any unit later receives a separate domain.

These decisions are deferred until the owner supplies or approves the required brand inputs.
