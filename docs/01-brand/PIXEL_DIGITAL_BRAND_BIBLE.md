# Pixel&Digital — Brand Bible

Status: Positioning, tagline, colour palette and type approved 2026-07-22 (ODR-001, ODR-013); logo remains interim raster pending vector masters (ODR-012)
Parent references: `docs/00-vision/PROJECT_BIBLE.md`, `docs/01-brand/BRAND_ARCHITECTURE.md`, `docs/01-brand/PIXEL_DIGITAL_LOGO_REGISTER.md`, `docs/08-governance/OWNER_DECISION_REGISTER.md`

## 1. Purpose

Define an actionable brand direction for the parent agency and its public experience without freezing final visual assets. Pixel&Digital must feel red-led, premium, modern and precise, and must turn creative authority into commercial confidence.

Official positioning: "Pixel & Digital est un studio créatif spécialisé dans la communication visuelle, le marketing digital, la création de contenus, l'audiovisuel, le développement web et les solutions d'impression à travers sa marque Kwaliti Print. Nous accompagnons les entreprises, institutions, ONG et entrepreneurs dans la création, le développement et la valorisation de leur image de marque."

Official tagline: "Nous créons des marques qui attirent, convainquent et restent en mémoire."

## 2. Principles

- Lead with clarity, evidence and purposeful energy.
- Express confidence through restraint, not decoration or inflated claims.
- Present strategy, creativity, technology and production as one coherent capability.
- Make every visual choice support comprehension, proof or action.
- Keep the parent brand visible when visitors move between business-unit worlds.

## 3. Concrete expression rules

### Visual language

- Red is the dominant signature. Approved palette (ODR-013, 2026-07-22): primary red `#E71E2D`, secondary dark red `#981D2A`, white `#FFFFFF`, black `#000000`. The palette may evolve later without architectural impact.
- Approved type (ODR-013): Outfit, as the single official typeface across headings, subheadings, body text, buttons and UI.
- Implementation direction: store colours and type as design tokens (with Kwaliti Print's own token set) rather than hard-coded component values, so the two brands stay swappable themes. See ODR-024 in `docs/08-governance/OWNER_DECISION_REGISTER.md`.
- Use controlled contrast, strong editorial hierarchy, deliberate whitespace and precise alignment.
- Prefer authentic work, people, processes and outcomes over generic stock imagery.
- Use graphic systems that suggest connection, transformation and momentum without visual noise.

### Voice and content

- Use concise, direct and business-aware language.
- Lead with client problems and outcomes, then explain capabilities and process.
- Support every material claim with a named proof type: result, deliverable, testimonial or documented method.
- Avoid unsupported superlatives, unexplained jargon and technology-first messaging.
- Keep calls to action explicit: discuss a need, request a quotation, submit a brief or view relevant work.

## 4. Boundaries

- Pixel&Digital must not become visually aggressive, trend-dependent or overloaded with effects.
- Premium does not mean sparse information, luxury clichés or inaccessible interaction.
- Red must not compromise contrast, reading comfort or action hierarchy.
- Do not make Studio or Training appear fully launched in the MVP; they remain labelled foundations or teasers.
- Do not publish invented results, client claims, prices, legal statements or availability.

## 5. Mobile behavior

- Preserve the promise, proof and primary action before decorative media.
- Reduce layout complexity and motion while retaining the red-led identity and editorial hierarchy.
- Keep controls comfortably operable and critical actions visible without obstructing content.
- Replace interactions dependent on hover, precision pointing or wide canvases with explicit controls and linear reading order.

## 6. Accessibility

- Validate contrast for every text, control and state; red alone must never carry meaning.
- Maintain visible focus, logical headings, keyboard access and descriptive link labels.
- Provide meaningful alternative text, captions or transcripts according to the media purpose.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences and preserve all information without animation, sound or 3D.
- Use plain language for services, forms, errors and next steps.

## 7. Performance

- Prioritize message, navigation and conversion controls before non-essential media.
- Treat large imagery, video, motion and 3D as budgeted editorial assets, not default page furniture.
- Reserve media space to prevent disruptive layout movement.
- Provide lightweight fallbacks and never block a journey while an enhancement loads.

## 8. Conversion intent

- Every page has one primary intent and at most a small number of contextual secondary actions.
- Place relevant evidence before high-commitment requests.
- Match the call to action to intent: exploration leads to related work; evaluation leads to process or proof; readiness leads to contact, brief or quotation.
- Explain what happens after form submission without promising an unapproved response time.
- Measure meaningful progression between service, proof and enquiry, subject to later analytics decisions.

## 9. Content implications

Required content inputs include a validated positioning statement, service hierarchy, process, team information, approved case studies, attributable outcomes, testimonials with permissions, and media rights metadata. Each item needs an owner, business-unit scope, review status and update responsibility. Missing proof must be exposed as a content gap, not filled with placeholder claims.
