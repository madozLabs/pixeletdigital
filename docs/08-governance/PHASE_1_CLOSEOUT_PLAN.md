# Phase 1 Closeout Plan

Status: Historical closeout sequence; Phase 2 architecture now authorized separately

## Fixed boundaries

- Pixel&Digital and Kwaliti Print are the live MVP worlds.
- Pixel&Digital services remain candidates until the owner approves the launch set and public names.
- Personalized Gadgets is the only current-stated Kwaliti Print capability; it is not yet publication-approved.
- Banner Printing, Vinyl Printing and 3D Lettering/CNC remain future-only and receive no current-capability page or quotation route.
- Studio and Training remain teaser/foundation only. Promotion to a full launch requires a separate owner-approved scope change.
- This historical plan does not itself authorize technical work; Phase 2 architecture is authorized separately by the completed entry gate. Production deployment remains excluded.

## Smallest practical sequence

| Order | Work | Owner responsibility | CTO responsibility | Acceptance criteria |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Freeze launch scope and boundaries: ODR-001 to ODR-011 and ODR-021 | Complete every decision field with an explicit disposition, wording/value, authority and evidence | Check completeness, contradictions and preservation of current/future and teaser boundaries | Positioning, launch sets, pricing, conversion approach, languages, geography, teaser treatment and Insights boundary are explicit; no candidate or future capability is silently promoted |
| 2 | Close brand, evidence and media inputs: ODR-012 to ODR-016 | Supply or approve authoritative brand sources/rules, publishable evidence, rights, media direction and budget | Verify provenance; distinguish repository evidence from approval; identify unsupported claims and missing rights | Every retained launch item has an accountable owner and approved factual scope; evidence/media is permissioned, or an evidence-light launch risk is explicitly accepted; observed raster evidence is not presented as an approved brand token |
| 3 | Close operational, legal and data inputs: ODR-017 to ODR-020 and ODR-022 | Decide launch timing; provide legal identity; name legal/privacy authority; approve privacy, consent, retention, analytics constraints and file handling | Check sufficiency for content/page freeze and record unresolved reversible constraints | Legal identity and accountable reviewer are recorded; privacy, consent, retention and file-handling rules are explicit; analytics and operational constraints are documented without selecting unapproved technology |
| 4 | Reconcile and freeze Phase 1 documentation | Approve corrections that alter business, brand, legal, budget or accepted-risk decisions | Update the decision register and all affected facts, catalogues, taxonomy, page mapping, journeys, wireframes, readiness and brand/governance documents; run a contradiction review | Approved answers are consistent downstream; each retained launch page has minimum approved content, ownership and conversion intent; unresolved assumptions remain visible and reversible; no invented claim, price, date, geography, right or legal fact remains |
| 5 | Perform the Phase 1 closeout review | Resolve returned decisions and sign the completed decision set | Reassess readiness and Phase 2 entry criteria against repository evidence; issue a pass/fail verdict and residual-risk list | The Phase 1 close condition is met, entry inputs are evidenced, documentation is consistent, and no blocking decision remains open or disguised as a default |
| 6 | Decide Phase 2 authorization separately | Complete the entry decision in `PHASE_2_ENTRY_CRITERIA.md` with scope, exclusions, accepted residual risks, approver and date | Confirm authorization is explicit and bounded; reject a generic instruction to continue | The Phase 2 entry decision is explicitly `Approved` and every authorization field is complete |

## Exact condition permitting Phase 2 authorization

Phase 2 is permitted only when all of the following are true:

1. Steps 1–5 pass their acceptance criteria and a documented Phase 1 pass verdict exists.
2. The MVP scope and boundaries are frozen: Pixel&Digital and Kwaliti Print are live; Personalized Gadgets retains only its approved current scope; Banner Printing, Vinyl Printing and 3D Lettering/CNC remain future-only; Studio and Training remain teaser/foundation only unless a separately approved scope change states otherwise.
3. Every retained launch item has an accountable owner, approved factual scope, minimum content, evidence/rights disposition and approved conversion route.
4. Legal identity, accountable legal/privacy review, privacy/consent/retention expectations, customer-file handling and relevant operational constraints are recorded.
5. Unresolved assumptions and residual risks are explicit and identified as reversible architecture constraints.
6. The project owner marks `Owner authorization: Approved` in `PHASE_2_ENTRY_CRITERIA.md` and completes the approved Phase 2 scope, explicit exclusions, accepted residual risks, approver and decision date.

These conditions remain the historical business-closeout standard. Phase 2 architecture was authorized separately on 2026-07-15 with unresolved items recorded as reversible risks; public release and production deployment remain blocked until their applicable conditions are satisfied.
