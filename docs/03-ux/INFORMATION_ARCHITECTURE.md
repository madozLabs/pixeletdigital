# Information Architecture — Pixel&Digital Platform

Status: MVP UX foundation; implementation-neutral
Parent references: `docs/02-product/MVP_SCOPE.md`, `docs/03-ux/PUBLIC_JOURNEYS.md`, `docs/03-ux/ADMIN_JOURNEYS.md`

## 1. Purpose

Define how public and internal information is grouped, named and connected so users can orient themselves, evaluate evidence and complete the next action. This document defines content and navigation requirements, not URLs, software structure or a technical stack.

## 2. Principles

- Organize public content around user intent and proof, not internal departments alone.
- Keep each brand world distinct while preserving ecosystem orientation and shared interaction logic.
- Give every content item one owner, one primary universe and explicit relationships.
- Keep public content separate from unpublished, commercial and operational information.
- Prefer shallow, predictable paths and strong contextual links over large undifferentiated menus.

## 3. Public MVP structure

### Pixel&Digital

- Home: promise, capability overview, selected proof, ecosystem entry and primary contact action.
- Agency: positioning, method, team and credibility.
- Services: service families, service detail, related evidence and enquiry action.
- Work: case-study index and detail with context, contribution, outputs and approved outcomes.
- Insights: resource index and detail, where sufficient governed content exists.
- Contact: general contact, project brief, quotation and consultation routes.
- Our worlds: Kwaliti Print plus clearly labelled Studio and Training teasers.

### Kwaliti Print

- Home: proposition, capability families, material proof and quotation entry.
- Capabilities: categories organized by need or application, with confirmed options and constraints.
- Work: relevant products, projects and fabrication examples.
- Materials and finishes: only validated guidance that supports selection.
- Quotation request: a staged, print-specific qualification path.
- Ecosystem link: persistent identification of the Pixel&Digital relationship and a clear return route.

### Studio and Training

MVP entries are limited to concise teasers or reserved foundations. They may state an approved purpose and offer a general information/contact route, but must not expose full catalogues, schedules, availability, registration or launch claims.

## 4. Workspace MVP structure

- Home: role-relevant actions, exceptions and recent activity.
- Content: pages, services, work, resources, testimonials, team and navigation.
- Media: assets, ownership, rights, alternatives and usages.
- Leads: incoming requests, qualification, ownership, next action and history.
- Users and access: users, roles and universe scopes for authorized roles.
- Brand settings: governed identity and public settings by universe.
- Activity: sensitive actions and relevant audit history.

Navigation and visibility must follow role and universe scope. Preview is an action. Content follows Brouillon, En revue, Planifié when needed, Publié and Archivé; rejection returns it to Brouillon with a review note.

## 5. Concrete rules and boundaries

- Every page exposes its current universe, subject and next meaningful action.
- Each service links to relevant work; each case study identifies contributing units and related services.
- Navigation labels use customer language and remain stable across devices.
- Search and filters support discovery but never replace a navigable hierarchy.
- A content object cannot silently serve as both public copy and private commercial record.
- Studio and Training cannot appear as complete MVP destinations without an approved scope change.
- Do not encode final URLs, domains, languages, prices or legal pages before owner decisions.

## 6. Mobile behavior

- Preserve the same hierarchy and labels as larger screens; reprioritize presentation rather than hiding essential destinations.
- Place universe identity, local navigation access and primary action within easy reach without covering content.
- Linearize comparisons and multi-column evidence in a meaningful reading order.
- Forms disclose purpose, expected information and progress; optional information remains optional.
- Cross-world transitions must offer a clear destination label and return route.

## 7. Accessibility

- Use unique, descriptive page titles, one clear primary heading and logical heading order.
- Provide landmarks, skip access, visible focus and keyboard-operable navigation.
- Links describe destinations; repeated actions include enough context to distinguish them.
- Status, validation and filter results are communicated beyond colour and visually announced state.
- Media relationships, captions and alternatives remain meaningful in reading order.

## 8. Performance

- Core navigation, headings, decision content and forms cannot depend on rich media or client capability.
- Limit navigation payload and avoid previews that require heavy media before intent is known.
- Load below-priority related content progressively without moving or blocking primary content.
- Rich transitions between worlds must preserve a direct, lightweight navigation path.

## 9. Conversion intent

- Discovery pages lead to evaluation; evaluation pages lead to proof; proof and service pages lead to the appropriate enquiry.
- Calls to action retain source page, universe and service context for qualification.
- Contact choices must explain their purpose without promising unapproved availability or timing.
- Dead ends are not allowed: every index, detail, empty state and teaser offers a relevant next step.

## 10. Content implications

The structure requires governed records for universes, pages, service families, services, case studies, contributing units, approved outcomes, resources, team, testimonials, media, navigation and form context. Each needs ownership, publication status, SEO fields where public, relationships and archival behavior. Content inventory and gap review must precede final navigation labels.

## 11. Open owner decisions

- Launch languages, geographies and final public terminology.
- Final service catalogue and grouping for each live universe.
- Pricing visibility and consultation-booking approach.
- Whether Insights launches with sufficient approved content.
- Final Studio and Training names, teaser wording and information route.
- Legal identity, required public pages, consent language and retention requirements.
- Any future separate-domain policy for business units.
