# Motion and Immersion Guidelines

Status: MVP UX foundation; implementation-neutral
Parent references: `docs/01-brand/BRAND_ARCHITECTURE.md`, `docs/03-ux/PUBLIC_JOURNEYS.md`

## 1. Purpose

Define when motion, media and 3D improve understanding, orientation, brand expression or evidence. The baseline experience must remain complete without them; 3D is progressive enhancement only.

## 2. Principles

- Meaning before spectacle; response before choreography.
- Motion explains change, hierarchy, causality or material transformation.
- Users remain in control of navigation, media and task completion.
- Brand worlds may use distinct rhythms while sharing predictable interaction behavior.
- Every enhancement has a lightweight, accessible and content-equivalent fallback.

## 3. Concrete rules

- Use entrance motion sparingly to establish hierarchy, never to delay readable content.
- Use transition motion to preserve origin and destination when changing page, state or universe.
- Give controls immediate feedback and keep state changes visually stable.
- Never hide essential instructions, controls or results until an animation completes.
- Do not autoplay sound. Any playing media has visible pause and mute controls.
- Do not require scrolling speed, cursor tracking, device tilt or gesture precision to reveal content.
- Looping motion must be subtle, pausable when material, and absent around long-form reading or forms.

### Brand rhythm

- Pixel&Digital: precise, energetic movement suggesting connection and transformation; avoid restless continuous motion.
- Kwaliti Print: measured reveals suggesting layers, cutting, folding, printing and assembly; never imply a production result inaccurately.
- Studio and Training: teasers only in MVP; motion must not imply a complete offer or launched destination.

### 3D admission test

Use 3D only when it clarifies form, material, construction, scale or a meaningful transformation better than lighter media. Before approval, identify its user value, fallback, loading behavior, input method, reduced-motion behavior and content owner. If any is missing, use static imagery, video or illustration instead.

## 4. Boundaries

- No essential navigation, product understanding, proof, form input or conversion may depend on motion, sound or 3D.
- No forced intro, scroll hijacking, hidden cursor, uncontrolled camera or motion that competes with reading.
- No decorative 3D without an explicit user or content purpose.
- No simulated product finish or outcome presented as documentary evidence.
- These guidelines do not select animation libraries, rendering methods, formats or delivery infrastructure.

## 5. Mobile behavior

- Default to fewer simultaneous effects, shorter travel and simpler scenes.
- Replace hover and pointer-following behavior with explicit tap controls or static presentation.
- Do not assume high bandwidth, high-end graphics capability, landscape orientation or continuous attention.
- Keep page movement and browser gestures conventional; avoid interactions that conflict with scrolling.
- Offer enhanced media after core content is available and preferably after user intent.

## 6. Accessibility

- Respect reduced-motion preferences by removing non-essential movement and replacing spatial transitions with immediate state changes or gentle fades.
- Provide pause, stop or hide controls for material moving content.
- Prevent rapid flashes, disorienting parallax, unexpected zoom and vestibular triggers.
- Preserve keyboard operation, focus location and screen-reader announcements through every state change.
- Caption spoken media, provide transcripts where needed and ensure visual information has a text equivalent.

## 7. Performance

- Core copy, navigation, evidence and actions load and function before enhancements.
- Define an editorial performance allowance for each rich asset during later architecture; absence of a budget means the asset is not approved for release.
- Avoid simultaneous autoplay media and unnecessary work outside the visible task.
- Reserve dimensions, show honest loading state and fail directly to the fallback.
- Test the experience without enhancements and on constrained mobile conditions before visual approval.

## 8. Conversion intent

- Motion may guide attention to the next step, demonstrate a process or strengthen proof, but cannot obstruct or repeatedly animate a call to action.
- Enhanced product views must keep quotation access and essential specifications available.
- World transitions must confirm destination and retain a clear return route.
- Measure interaction only when it answers a business or usability question; engagement with an effect is not conversion by itself.

## 9. Content implications

Every rich asset needs a purpose statement, owning universe, source and rights, poster or static fallback, alternative or transcript, crop guidance, and review status. 3D also needs an accuracy owner and approved representation of material or object. Motion concepts must be reviewable as storyboards before asset production to avoid premature cost.

## 10. Open owner decisions

- Sound identity policy and whether sound is used at all.
- Media-production and original-3D budget.
- Approved product, workshop, team and case-study assets.
- Desired intensity of each brand’s motion within these constraints.
- Final Studio and Training identities before any world-specific motion system.
- Later measurable performance allowances after architecture and hosting decisions.
